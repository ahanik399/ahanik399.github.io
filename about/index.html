About the Bureau

Insight Gaps

An independent public-interest research bureau focused on surfacing hidden operational patterns through forensic data analysis.

Most operational failure is not dramatic. It is quiet, structural, and accumulative — invisible to standard reporting until it has already compounded.

Insight Gaps exists to make those patterns visible.

Editorial Independence

No corporate funding. No advertising. No paywalls on core investigations. The Bureau's analytical conclusions are not for sale — they are derived from data, following the evidence wherever it leads.

We do not accept funding from organizations we investigate or analyze. We do not take positions on behalf of clients in public reporting.

What We Publish

Insight Gaps publishes two categories of work:

Public investigations are fully open-access forensic reports examining systemic operational issues — available to any reader, journalist, or researcher.

Applied analysis translates investigative findings into operational intelligence — structured forensic reports on specific domains, available publicly and as the basis for commissioned private engagements.

Commissioned Work

The Bureau accepts a limited number of private operational diagnostics for organizations facing complex documentation or workflow failures. These private engagements follow the exact same methodology and independence standards as our public investigations.

Commissioned work is delivered under strict confidentiality. No client names, data, or findings from private engagements are disclosed publicly.
