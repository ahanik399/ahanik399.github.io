Methods & Standards

How we investigate documentation failures and extract operational truth from complex, real-world data.

Last updated: January 2026

The credibility of forensic data journalism depends entirely on its methodology. We document ours openly — so that findings can be challenged, reproduced, and built upon.

Editorial Standards

All published work — investigations and applied analysis — must meet the following standards before release:

  • Verifiability: Findings must be traceable to documented, replicable data sources.
  • Anonymization: Where real operational data is used, all identifying information is removed or aggregated. No individuals, clients, vendors, or properties are identifiable in published work.
  • Independence: Analytical conclusions are not shaped by commercial relationships or funding. No findings are modified, suppressed, or withheld to protect any organization.
  • Proportionality: Findings are stated in proportion to the evidence. We do not overstate certainty or extrapolate beyond what the data supports.

Data Sources & Anonymization

Insight Gaps draws on multiple source categories depending on the investigation:

Aggregated operational records — anonymized work order histories, documentation submission logs, and rework event patterns drawn from real preservation workflows. No proprietary client data is disclosed.

Industry standards documentation — published requirements from major servicers (MCS, Safeguard, ServiceLink), HUD guidelines, and regulatory filings.

Simulated and synthetic data — in cases where live data cannot be used, analytically consistent synthetic datasets are constructed to illustrate patterns. These are always disclosed clearly.

Analytical Framework

Our operational diagnostics follow a consistent pattern-first methodology: rather than starting from a hypothesis and seeking confirmation, we start from raw activity data and surface structural patterns.

The primary analytical questions we ask of any dataset:

  • Where does value fail to complete its journey?
  • What recurring patterns produce disproportionate loss?
  • At what point in the workflow does friction emerge?
  • Is the observed issue structural, environmental, or executional?

Commissioned Work Standards

Private commissioned diagnostics follow identical analytical and editorial standards to public investigations. The only difference is confidentiality: no commissioned findings, client identities, or data are disclosed publicly.

Commissioned clients do not have editorial influence over findings. Reports reflect the data — not the client's preferred conclusions.

Corrections Policy

If a factual error is identified in any published work, it will be corrected promptly and the correction will be noted in the document with the date of amendment. We do not silently edit published reports.
